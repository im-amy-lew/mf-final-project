About Us

The Sacramento Weavers' and Spinners' Guild (SWSG) was founded in 1946 with the purpose of encouraging growth in the art of weaving, spinning, and dyeing by means of exchange of ideas, reasearch, and contacts with experts in the field. The guild presents members with the opportunity to meet and share through workshops and lectures.

Specialty Groups

SWSG has multiple specialty group meetings in addition to the main guild meetings held on the fourth Tuesday of each month:

The Spinners' Group meets throughout the year on the first Tuesday of each month from 10am to 2pm. The usual location is Northminster Presbyterian Church (3235 Pope Avenue, Sacramento) but occasionally meets at other locations as announced in the guild newletter. Members provide their own lunch and beverage.

For more information, contact Josie Barnes.

The Weavers' Study Group meets throughout the year on the second Thursday of each month from 9:30am to noon at the Shepard Garden and Arts Center (3300 McKinley Blvd, Sacramento). The group chooses a particular weave or theme which is the topic of discussion for the meetings.

For more information, contact Langdon Faust.

The Saturday Group normally meets the second and fourth Saturdays of each month from 10:30am to 12:30pm on Zoom. Meeting information is posted in the guild newsletter.

For more information, contact Susan Werrin.
